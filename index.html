A Hull of A Project

The PEP (Promoting Electric Propulsion) Competition, hosted by the American Society of Naval Engineers, is a national event where schools build electric boats with autonomous capabilities. In 2023, W&M's team scored third place, and I went on to captain the team for the 2024 competition.


Our boat was built from scratch, featuring a wooden hull made from plywood, fiberglass, and tons of flotation foam.


The specifications of the boat were as follows:

  • Dimensions: 6 ft x 4 ft x 3 ft
  • Weight: 600 lbs
  • Power Source: 4S2P @ 48V nominal
  • Motor: 1.5kW brushless motors
  • Propulsion: 8 inch 3-blade propellers
  • Electronics: CubeOrange, GPS, Magnetometer, etc.

A Thrifting Revolution

T'Swap is a venture I co-founded with Jack Rizzo during our freshman year. Our goal was to enable William & Mary students to thrift affordably and sustainably. We created a system where students could donate clothes for T'Swap credit, which they used to purchase new items. With no upfront costs and minimal expenses, we generated over $1,500 in profit through just six open shops.


In 2024, we expanded our impact by partnering with various clubs and organizations on campus. These collaborations allowed us to share profits, enabling clubs to raise funds for their causes while promoting themselves through our social media reach. This unique approach helped clubs attract new members and increase their visibility.
